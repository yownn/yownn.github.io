Fun Facts

Slowest Swimmer

Dwarf Seahorse

The dwarf seahorse is a species of seahorse found in the subtidal aquatic beds of the Bahamas and parts of the United States. According to Guinness World Records, it is the slowest-moving fish, with a top speed of about 5 feet per hour.

A Living Fossil

Coelacanth

The coelacanths are members of a now-rare order of fish that includes two extant species in the genus Latimeria. Coelacanths are known from the fossil record dating back over 360 million years, with a peak in abundance about 240 million years ago. Before 1938 they were believed to have become extinct approximately 80 million years ago, when they disappeared from the fossil record.

Largest Fish in the Sea?!

Whale Shark

The whale shark (Rhincodon typus) earns the name “whale” solely because of its size. Just as the blue whale (Balaenoptera musculus) is the largest living mammal*, the whale shark is the largest species of any fish, known to reach more than 40 feet in length.


Strongest Boxer?

Mantis Shrimp

The miniweight boxing title of the animal world belongs to the mantis shrimp, a cigar-sized crustacean with front claws that can deliver an explosive 60-mile-per-hour punch. The speed of the shrimp's strike has been compared to that of a bullet leaving the barrel of a gun.
